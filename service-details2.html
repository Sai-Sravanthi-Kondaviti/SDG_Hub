Statistics

Around 690 million people (9.2% of the global population) suffer from hunger, with malnutrition contributing to 3.1 million child deaths annually.
24% of global greenhouse gas emissions come from agriculture, making sustainable practices crucial.
1 in 5 people in rural areas lack food security, and 1/3 of all food produced (about 1.3 billion tons) is wasted each year.
These figures highlight the need for urgent action to end hunger, improve food security, and promote sustainable agriculture.

Zero Hunger aims to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture. It focuses on ensuring access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food for all, especially the poor and vulnerable. The goal also emphasizes supporting small-scale farmers, improving agricultural productivity, and ensuring sustainable food production systems. Reducing food waste and addressing climate change impacts on agriculture are key aspects of this goal. Zero Hunger – Nearly 600 million people are projected to be undernourished by 2030.
