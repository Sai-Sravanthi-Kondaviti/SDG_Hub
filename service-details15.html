Statistics

As of 2023, deforestation continues at an alarming rate, with around 10 million hectares lost annually.
Nearly 75% of the Earth's land surface has been degraded due to human activities, impacting biodiversity and ecosystem services.
Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction, driven by habitat loss, climate change, and pollution.

Life on Land focuses on protecting, restoring, and promoting the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, managing forests sustainably, combating desertification, halting and reversing land degradation, and halting biodiversity loss. Healthy land ecosystems are essential for providing food, clean water, biodiversity, and livelihoods for people across the world. SDG 15 addresses the need for sustainable land use and conservation to ensure that these resources are available for future generations.
