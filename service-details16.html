Statistics

As of the latest data, SDG 16 faces significant challenges globally.
Around 1 in 4 people live in countries affected by violent conflict, with conflict-related deaths rising in many regions.
Corruption remains a pervasive issue, with over 40% of people globally reporting bribery incidents when accessing public services.
Judicial systems in many countries are weak, with only 50% of countries providing free legal aid.

Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions focuses on promoting peaceful, just, and inclusive societies, ensuring that everyone has access to justice, and building effective, accountable institutions at all levels. It addresses issues related to violence, corruption, inequality, and lack of transparency in institutions, recognizing the importance of good governance for achieving sustainable development.

Top 5 Key Problem Statements for SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions:

  • Weak Legal and Justice Systems
  • Corruption and Lack of Transparency in Government Institutions
  • High Levels of Violence and Insecurity
  • Limited Access to Effective and Inclusive Public Services
  • Low Public Participation and Citizen Engagement
