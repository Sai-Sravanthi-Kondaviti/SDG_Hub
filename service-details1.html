Statistics

As of 2021, 9.2% of the global population, or approximately 700 million people, live in extreme poverty, surviving on less than $1.90 a day.
The COVID-19 pandemic has reversed some progress,pushing an additional 100 million people into extreme poverty.
Around 46% of the global population had access to social protection in 2020, but 60% still lacked adequate coverage.
Sub-Saharan Africa remains the region with the highest poverty rates, with 77% of the world's poor living in rural areas.
Additionally, 1.3 billion people suffer from multidimensional poverty, which includes lack of education, poor health, and inadequate living standards.
Women and girls are particularly vulnerable to poverty.

No Poverty is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) established by the United Nations in 2015. The goal aims to eradicate extreme poverty for all people everywhere by 2030, and to reduce the proportion of men, women, and children living in poverty in all its dimensions.
